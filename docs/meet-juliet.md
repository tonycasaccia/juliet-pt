# Meet Juliet St. Germain

Juliet was born and raised in Albuquerque, New Mexico.

She earned her B.S. in Physiology & Exercise Science from the University of New Mexico and her Doctorate in Physical Therapy from Northern Arizona University, graduating with distinction.

She is published in research on resistance training, hypertrophy, and the physiological effects of mask wearing during exercise, and plans to remain actively involved in clinical research and presentation.

---

## Clinical Training & Philosophy

Juliet's treatment philosophy follows an integrative whole-systems approach influenced by the Institute of Physical Art and the Barral Institute.

After completing the rigorous process to become a Certified Functional Manual Therapist (CFMT), she works with both non-athletes and athletes ranging from recreational to Olympic level across sports including cycling, running, swimming, triathlon, soccer, and baseball.

She also serves on medical teams providing acute orthopedic care at the Western States 100 Mile ultramarathon.

See more about treatment philosophy here:
https://instituteofphysicalart.com/

---

## Aquatic Therapy Leadership

Juliet is the director and developer of the Aquatic Wellness and Therapy program at Menlo Swim and Sport.

She values connection within community and expanding access to care through group-based and one-on-one aquatic rehabilitation.

---

## Outside the Clinic

Outside of practice, she enjoys trail and road cycling, running, and swimming — and embraces being a beginner in new experiences, from wine tasting to Flamenco dancing.
